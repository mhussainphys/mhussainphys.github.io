ABOUT

I am a graduate of the University of Chicago, currently working at Fermilab on the CMS MIP Timing Detector and the Fermilab Quantum Network Experiment. My research interests include beyond standard model physics, pile-up mitigation techniques, machine learning in high energy physics, software and computing architecture development for high energy physics, detector and sensor R&D, quantum technologies, and cross disciplinary research -- particularly at the intersection of high energy physics and quantum networking. Besides research, I am interested in long distance running, mountaineering sports, bouldering, skyrunning, and alpine climbing. I follow these areas closely, and I am trying to venture out in as many of them as possible.
